What Was Erased

The World State did not destroy the past carelessly. It erased it with precision — identifying what threatened stability and removing it, leaving no visible wound. These ten exhibits document what was taken and the traces that survived.

On what basis do we reconstruct a past that was deliberately destroyed?

What Remains

Archeology is the process of working with what remains, and I wanted to explore what happens when the remains are erased or curated. This project was interesting to me because I was analyzing the future with a missing past, with the past in the novel being our present today. The past haunts the present and the present haunts the future, so it was interesting to consider why the World State might choose to remove certain things, and what remains even after attempts at erasure. When analyzing from an Archeological Imagination perspective, I found myself largely reflecting on what it means to be human. In Brave New World, the World State attempts to make people as similar as possible, removing individuality. Additionally, a lot of what they aim to erase (music, poetry, Shakespeare, God) are linked to provoking emotion. Many of these emotions are also linked to memory. For example, in the case of music, songwriters share their memories as music which provokes emotion in others. As such, I would argue that embracing emotion and our memories are a large part of what makes us human. The past is part of what makes us human too: the past, and working with what remains, is an especially strong way to provoke emotion and memory.
